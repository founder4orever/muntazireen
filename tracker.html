Welcome

Muntazireen.
Those who prepare.

This tracker is your system. Five weeks of structured preparation across three pillars — Mind, Body, and Survival. Each pillar builds on the last. After five weeks, maintenance begins — and never ends.

Go at your own pace. Check tasks off when done. Missed tasks carry forward. Nothing resets except your intention.

Pillar One

Mind.
Deen first.

Everything built here starts with the foundation of iman and knowledge. The Mind pillar covers your daily prayers and their consistency, Quranic learning (Ayatul Kursi and the first 10 ayat of Al-Kahf — both directly connected to the signs we are preparing for), and building your understanding of Aqeedah through Kitab at-Tawheed.

A body prepared without a grounded mind is just adrenaline. The mind is the anchor.

Pillar Two

Body.
Your body is amanah.

Physical readiness is not vanity — it is responsibility. The Body pillar builds cardiovascular endurance, strength, and weaponry skills week by week. You will be asked about your current fitness level and your preferred weapons — the tracker builds your daily and weekly plan around your answers.

Week 3 onwards, weaponry training becomes a daily drill. Your choices — knife, archery, firearm, or hand combat — define your sessions.

Pillar Three

Survival.
Prepare the earth around you.

The Survival pillar covers everything physical outside your body — gear acquisition, safe zones, first aid, food storage, water filtration, radiation monitoring, and emergency planning. Each item is tiered — Tier 1 first, then building outward.

First aid training is built in week by week, mirroring a certified first aid curriculum. You will also be asked how you want to complete it — through our system or an external certification of your choice.

The Development Phase

Five weeks.
Your pace.

The Development Phase is five weeks of structured daily and weekly tasks across all three pillars. Weeks advance by calendar — but missed tasks carry forward into a separate section and stay until you complete them. Gear that hasn't been acquired stays in your queue.

After Week 5, you enter Maintenance Mode — permanent daily and weekly habits that continue indefinitely. There is no endpoint. Preparation is not a phase, it is a way of life.
